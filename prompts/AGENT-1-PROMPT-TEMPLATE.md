...existing code...

- Common baseline practices

- Variations and divergences
- Best practices that should be standardized
- Gaps and inconsistencies

## Tasks

### 1. Document Inventory

Create a complete inventory of all provided documents:

- **Rig name**
- **Document type** (ROP, JSA, Checklist, etc.)
- **Operation covered** (BOP Installation, Testing, Removal, etc.)
- **Version/revision** (if stated)
- **Date** (if stated)
- **Page count** or section count
- **Key sections identified**

Format as a table for clarity.

### 2. Structure Mapping

Map the organizational structure of each document:

- **Main sections** (e.g., Preparation, Execution, Testing)
- **Sub-sections and annexes**
- **Risk assessment sections**
- **Equipment lists**
- **Permit-to-work references**
- **Critical steps/hold points**
- **JSA structure** (steps, hazards, controls)

Identify which rigs follow similar structures and which diverge.

### 3. Detailed Comparison by Phase/Step

Perform a **line-by-line comparison** of procedures across all rigs:

#### For ROPs

**For each operational phase** (Preparation, Rigging, Installation, Testing, etc.):

1. **Common steps**: List steps that appear in all or most rigs
2. **Variations**: Identify where rigs differ in:
   - Step order
   - Specific actions
   - Verification requirements
   - Equipment used
   - Personnel roles
3. **Missing steps**: Note where some rigs have steps that others lack
4. **Best practices**: Highlight superior approaches (e.g., better verification, clearer instructions, stronger controls)

#### For JSAs

**For each step in the job sequence**:

1. **Hazards identified**: Compare hazard lists across rigs
2. **Control measures**: Compare controls for equivalent hazards
3. **Risk ratings**: Note differences in risk assessment
4. **Critical controls**: Identify which rigs mark certain controls as critical
5. **Verification methods**: Compare how controls are verified

### 4. Equipment and Technical Requirements

Compare:

- **BOP specifications** (type, pressure ratings, dimensions)
- **Lifting equipment** (cranes, slings, shackles)
- **Testing equipment** (pressure test units, flow meters)
- **Tools and accessories**
- **Instrumentation**

Identify commonalities and rig-specific requirements.

### 5. Permits, Authorizations, and Critical Lifts

Compare requirements for:

- **Permit-to-work** systems
- **Critical lift planning**
- **Hot work permits** (if applicable)
- **Simultaneous operations (SIMOPS)** protocols
- **Authority levels** for sign-offs

### 6. Summary of Best Practices and Divergences

Provide a concise summary:

- **Top 5 best practices** observed (with rig examples)
- **Top 5 areas of divergence** requiring standardization
- **Critical safety gaps** where standardization is most urgent
- **Areas where rig-specific procedures may be necessary** (due to equipment or operational differences)

## Quality Standards

- **Be exhaustive**: Don't skip steps or sections
- **Be specific**: Use exact terminology from the documents
- **Be objective**: Present facts, not opinions
- **Be comparative**: Always show how rigs differ or align
- **Be actionable**: Provide insights that enable standardization decisions
